Chocolate Chip Cookies

A chocolate dessert

Crispy edges, soft centers, and packed with chocolate chips.

History of Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies were invented in the 1930s by Ruth Wakefield at the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts. She added chopped chocolate to cookie dough expecting it to melt, but instead it held its shape — creating one of the most popular cookies in the world.

Recipe & Ingredients

  • Ingredients
  • White sugar
  • Brown sugar
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla extract
  • Flour
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Chocolate chips
  • Butter
  • Walnuts
  • Chocolate chunks
  • Milk

Directions:

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • Cream the butter and both sugars together.
  • Mix in the eggs and vanilla.
  • Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt.
  • Fold in the chocolate chips.
  • Place spoonfuls of dough on a baking sheet.
  • Bake for 10–12 minutes until golden brown.
